The brand

The dominant AU incumbent, now in defensive mode after years of brand-relevance pressure.

Nike leads Australia's sportswear market at 17% value share (Euromonitor / IMARC, 2025), backed by global athlete endorsements, deep wholesale and DTC presence, and consistent high-spend above-the-line advertising. It competes directly with Lululemon in women's training and running categories.

Globally and in AU, Nike is navigating a brand relevance challenge in 2024-25, responding with heavier media investment. Its first dedicated AU brand campaign in a decade ("Never Done Inspiring") launched recently, signalling a defensive reinvestment rather than organic growth.

Why it matters to Lululemon

Nike's 51.2% share of search is the category ceiling Lululemon operates beneath. Its AU-native campaign relaunch narrows the aspiration and community positioning that Lululemon has owned. Nike losing 2.7 percentage points of SoS is not flowing to Lululemon: Adidas is absorbing it. That structural shift is the more important signal.
