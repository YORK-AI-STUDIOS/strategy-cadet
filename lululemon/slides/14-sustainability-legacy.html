The legacy play: movement as infrastructure for the next generation’s wellbeing.

Brand DNA · Sustainability
Probable on Be Well agenda
Long-term impact

Normalise active, mindful, connected life as the default human operating state.

Lululemon’s stated Be Well impact agenda targets carbon neutrality, circular product design, and fair supply chain practices. In FY2024 the brand hit 77% recycled polyester (beating its own 75% target), achieved a 77% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions, and ran 100% renewable electricity across owned sites.

For the next generation of customers, the ambition reaches beyond environmental metrics. The brand aims to normalise mindfulness and active living as daily behaviours, not occasional pursuits. Physical wellbeing, mental health, and community belonging are framed as a single system.

If it succeeds, the legacy is a cohort of people who inherit both better-made technical apparel and a culture that treats movement as fundamental to human flourishing, not a luxury wellness add-on.

Five words, ethical practice

Responsibility expressed as materials commitment, not marketing.

Circular. Mindful. Transparent. Inclusive. Regenerative.

Lululemon’s sustainability narrative is grounded in material science and supply chain: proprietary fabric R&D, circular product programs (Like New re-commerce, in-store repairs confirmed at the Emporium Melbourne flagship), and a stated goal of preferred materials covering 90% of products by 2030. The Texas AG PFAS investigation (April 2026) is the live reputational test of whether these commitments hold.
