Excess Share of Voice, brand by brand. Adidas is buying share. Lululemon is in expansion on mindshare leverage. Nike and Lorna Jane are defending.

Method · Binet ESOV framework
Probable
Excess Share of Voice (ESOV) is a brand's share of advertising voice minus its estimated share of market: ESOV = Share of Voice − Share of Market. A positive ESOV, where voice exceeds share, correlates with market-share growth. A negative ESOV, where share exceeds voice, signals a brand coasting on installed base.
Under-indexed
Voice trails share or recognition. Vulnerable to louder rivals replicating the position.
Expansion
Voice runs well ahead of share. The posture of a brand pushing for structural gains.
Defense
Voice broadly proportionate to share. Calibrated to hold an established position, not to grow it.
No AU activewear-specific SOV data is published. Classifications are directional, drawn from observed spend direction, market share estimates, and brand activity.
LululemonThis report
Expansion
Holds 9.1% AU fitness clothing market share (Ragtrader, 2025) but commands outsized mindshare through premium retail placement, 2,000+ global ambassadors, and consistent AU earned media. Over 80% of 2024 global marketing spend deployed to performance digital channels measured on customer lifetime value. Mindshare exceeds physical footprint.
Adidas
Expansion
10% AU market share with documented marketing spend +12% in most recent period, further increases planned for 2025. AU women's sport and athleisure lines outperforming Nike on creative effectiveness. The most active share-buyer in the category. Taking Nike's lost ground, not Lululemon's.
Nike
Defense
Market leader at 17% AU share, sustaining position through heavyweight above-the-line and sports sponsorships. Facing brand relevance pressure in 2024-25, responding with heavier media investment. First AU brand campaign in a decade signals defensive reinvestment. SoS decelerating -2.7pp.
Lorna Jane
Defense
Dominant AU-born brand, nearly 100 stores and $166M revenue (2024). Deliberately low-cost marketing relying on owned social and influencer content rather than paid media. Defending with community and product credibility, not outspend. TikTok always-on model delivers 4x ROAS without heavy paid investment.
Under Armour
Under-indexed
Global turnaround under CEO Kevin Plank, marketing spend deliberately flat in "micro doses." AU partnership with ISC Sport (800,000+ athletes, 2,500+ clubs) is grassroots, not above-the-line. Share of voice significantly lags AU market potential. SoS stalled at 2.9%.
P.E Nation
Under-indexed
Strong editorial brand (Vogue AU, RUSSH, marie claire) but limited paid media investment at scale. David Jones and THE ICONIC wholesale distribution. Voice distinctive but does not match potential share in AU's growing athleisure category. Watch item: SoS +100% in 3 months from small base.
The Upside
Under-indexed
Luxury niche positioning (Net-A-Porter, FWRD) limits TAM in AU but gives high credibility per impression. Media footprint concentrated in premium editorial, no significant paid media at scale confirmed. Premium positioning and limited voice leaves it exposed to share erosion from below. SoS flat at 1.1%.
Read across
Five of seven brands sit in Defense or Under-indexed. Only Adidas and Lululemon are in Expansion. The structural risk: Adidas is absorbing Nike's lost share, not Lululemon. The category conversation is shifting toward sport-performance crossover, Adidas's lane, not premium athleisure.
