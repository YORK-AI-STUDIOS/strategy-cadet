Audience 1, Urban Professional Women, Aged 28 to 44. Aspirational wellness-driven, quality over volume.

Profile
Verified
Core age band
28 – 44
Peak engagement in the 30 to 39 bracket, with strong representation up to 44.
Lululemon’s founding demographic and still its largest revenue cohort. Approximately 70% of Lululemon’s overall customer base is women. University-educated, professionally employed, earning AUD 100,000 to 180,000+ household income, living in inner-city Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane and Perth precincts. Sees fitness as a non-negotiable self-care ritual. Values quality over volume, buys fewer items at premium price points, and seeks clothing that transitions from studio to work to social without changing.

Lifestyle and values
Sees fitness as a non-negotiable self-care ritual. Values quality over volume, buys fewer items at premium price points. Seeks clothing that transitions from studio to work to social without changing. Highly engaged with wellness as identity, not just activity. Environmentally aware but not activist.
  • Studio community membership functions as social currency. Wearing Lululemon to Pilates is a participation signal within a defined social group.
  • Cost-per-wear logic justifies premium pricing. Price sensitivity increases outside core product lines (accessories, footwear).
