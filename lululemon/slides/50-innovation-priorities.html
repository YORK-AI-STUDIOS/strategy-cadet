Five priority moves, sequenced. Build the community moat first; build the men’s platform and circular commerce last.

Synthesis · Cross-audience
Uncertain
The ideation.md does not rank its concepts, so this sequence is a Strategy Cadet synthesis. It orders the five concepts by two tests: how many audiences a move serves, and how quickly it can ship. The SweatLife Hub and Fit Intelligence moves serve all three audiences and have the shortest build cycle; the Men’s Collective and Loop and Lift require deeper infrastructure investment and are sequenced accordingly.
Move 01 · Now01

Launch SweatLife Local Hubs.

The community moat that no competitor has replicated at scale in Australia. Converts store square metres from transactional retail into retention infrastructure. One concept, three distinct audience implementations: professional women’s programming, men’s performance collectives, Gen Z co-designed events.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 02 · Now02

Ship Precision Fit Intelligence.

The single highest-impact conversion tool across all channels. Reduces return rates for professional women, lowers purchase friction for Gen Z, and provides performance rationale for male athletes. Fit uncertainty is the primary reason premium activewear shoppers abandon carts or over-order and return.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 03 · Next03

Build the Men’s Collective.

The highest-ceiling growth bet in the idea bank. The men’s activewear opportunity is real and the window is closing: Vuori, On, and Pas Normal Studios are actively recruiting Lululemon’s natural male audience. A brand-within-a-brand move requiring content, community, and store investment.

Serves
Male athletesGen Z menWomen (gifting)
Move 04 · Next04

Launch Transparent Performance.

The trust architecture that defends premium pricing against a category of increasingly sophisticated sceptics. SKU-level proof for professional women, performance data for male athletes, social-native factory transparency for Gen Z. A brand claiming premium must be able to show its working.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 05 · Later05

Build Loop and Lift at scale.

The circular commerce repositioning: from garment brand to closed-loop quality ecosystem. Highest infrastructure requirement (certified resale platform, trade-in logistics, AU compliance), so sequenced after the first four moves establish revenue to fund it. Lululemon’s Like New programme exists in the US; AU launch is an extension, not a greenfield build.

Serves
All three audiences
