Audience 3, Gen Z Active Explorers, Aged 18 to 27. Appearance and community-oriented, brand-loyal when introduced early.

Profile
Probable
Core age band
18 – 27
Core cluster 20 to 25. University students, early-career workers and those in the first 3 to 5 years of employment.
Mixed but female-skewed: approximately 60% female, 40% male and gender-diverse, reflecting Lululemon’s increasing gender-neutral positioning and Gen Z’s different approach to category boundaries. Lower discretionary spend (AUD 25,000 to 65,000) but strong aspirational spend on identity-aligned categories. Despite making up a third of the adult population, Gen Z drives 41% of annual wellness spend (McKinsey 2025). BNPL and gifting normalise premium purchases on lower incomes.

Wellness framing
Appearance primary, framed as wellnessValues alignment with brands matters but is held lightly. BNPL normalises aspirational purchases on lower incomes.
Lifestyle and values
Appearance and aesthetic are primary, framed through wellness and performance language. Social proof through peer and influencer networks drives product discovery. Community belonging is a core motivator for joining fitness formats.
  • The purchase often precedes the commitment: buying the Align leggings before consistently attending Pilates. Lululemon is the aspiration that scaffolds the behaviour.
  • Brand-loyal when introduced early: first purchase often leads to repeat purchase as income grows. Acquiring this cohort now is a long-term lifetime value play.
