Three words, founder’s spirit
Obsessive. Counter-cultural. Aspirational.
Less “build another sportswear brand,” more “build the apparel that serious practitioners of movement actually need, starting from the fabric up.”

Founding story

Chip Wilson founded Lululemon in 1998 in Vancouver after attending a yoga class and recognising that athletic apparel designed for women’s movement, particularly yoga, did not exist at a technical level. The category offered cotton gym wear and repurposed swimsuit fabric. Neither performed.

He built a brand around purpose-designed fabric and community-led retail to fill that gap, opening the first standalone store in November 2000. The founding insight was materials-first: solve the function and fit problem at fabric level before designing a garment.

Lululemon was not incubated inside a conglomerate. It was built by a founder with a specific body-movement problem and a materials obsession, in direct response to a category gap no incumbent had bothered to close.
