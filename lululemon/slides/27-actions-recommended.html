Five things to act on now, sequenced for the next twelve months.

Strategy · Actions 1 to 5
Probable
These five actions are the operational expression of the four pillars. Order matters. Community infrastructure (Action 1) and fabric science credibility (Action 2) are the foundational and trust layers. Circular acceleration (Action 3) and men’s expansion (Action 4) are the growth levers that only compound once the foundation holds. Action 5 closes the digital loop that currently lets the in-store educator model leak value online.
Q101

Deepen local community infrastructure in Australia.

What. Invest in regional studio partnerships and locally contextualised SweatLife events in Brisbane, Perth, and Adelaide growth corridors.

Why. AU footprint is strong in capital cities but the ambassador and event ecosystem is thin outside Melbourne and Sydney. Closes the gap between brand awareness and genuine community belonging.

Q1 - Q202

Make fabric science a content pillar.

What. Sustained content series spanning social, e-commerce, and in-store that translates proprietary fabric decisions (Luon, Nulu, Everlux) into plain-language customer benefits.

Why. The R&D story is under-narrated in market. Reinforces the price premium with proof over positioning. Creates a differentiation narrative competitors cannot quickly replicate.

Q2 - Q303

Accelerate circular product programs in Australia.

What. Localise and promote the Like New re-commerce program and product repair initiatives consistently across AU, closing the gap between AU and North American availability.

Why. Converts the brand’s stated circularity values into verifiable AU market behaviour. Addresses the growing Australian consumer expectation around sustainability with actual product infrastructure, not messaging.

Q3 - Q404

Expand the men’s category with community-led storytelling.

What. Build a men’s community layer via run clubs, training events, and male ambassador profiles in the AU market.

Why. The ABC pant has created a genuine men’s foothold but community infrastructure in Australia skews toward female-coded studio culture. Run clubs and male ambassador content accelerate category growth without product repositioning.

Q4 onward05

Invest in personalised digital discovery at purchase entry.

What. A fit-and-activity diagnostic tool integrated into the AU e-commerce experience, extending the educator model to digital-first customers.

Why. The in-store educator model is a proven conversion asset but the online experience does not replicate it. Reduces return rates and increases average order value by surfacing the right product for the right use case.
