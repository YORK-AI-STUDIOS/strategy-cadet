Theme 2: Community is earned through showing up, and it holds you when you do.

Brand DNA · Messaging theme 2 of 3
Probable
Hook
SweatLife is not a slogan. It is a behavioural contract. The brand’s community messaging is most credible when it shows the texture of real belonging.
The early morning class. The ambassador who remembers your name. The event that happened whether it rained or not. This theme exists because community is both the brand’s strongest growth mechanism and its most under-leveraged asset outside Melbourne and Sydney. The 2,000+ global ambassador network is the infrastructure; the AU/NZ localisation is the gap.
01Ambassador content

Hyper-local ambassador series, city by city.

Content profiling studio partners in specific Australian cities (Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane), showing their communities rather than their aesthetics. The Lewis Hamilton AU Grand Prix Emporium Melbourne activation (March 2026) is the high-profile proof-of-concept; the local ambassador series is the always-on version.

Reduces reliance on polished brand production while increasing local credibility.

Channel · Social · Owned · In-store
02CRM

Event-led CRM sequences with community-specific product follow-up.

Invite local customers to free community runs via the SweatLife calendar, then follow up with community-specific product recommendations. The “Be Well, Be Together” campaign (October 2025, 60+ free classes globally) is the existing platform to extend.

Converts event attendance into a CRM data point that makes subsequent product communications feel earned, not broadcast.

Channel · CRM · Email · Events
03Social

UGC-forward social strategy amplifying community movement content.

Community members’ own movement content amplified in-market, reducing reliance on polished brand production. The @lululemonausnz Instagram account (405K followers) is the existing distribution asset.

UGC-forward strategy directly addresses the “fast-fashion quality at luxury prices” editorial narrative (Grants Pass Tribune, Sept 2024) by surfacing real customers who genuinely love the product.
