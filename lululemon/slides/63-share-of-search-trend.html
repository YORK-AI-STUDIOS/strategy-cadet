Seasonal pattern (Lululemon)

Lululemon shows two AU-specific peaks: May (EOFY gifting) and November (Black Friday / Christmas). October is the annual trough, consistent with AU retail seasonal norms. The -0.3pp 12-month vs 3-month delta is within statistical noise, not a meaningful decline. The October window offers counter-seasonal brand-building opportunity at lower auction cost.

Implications for strategy
1The Nike-to-Adidas shift is the category signal.Nike shedding 2.7pp while Adidas gains 1.5pp. Lululemon is not capturing Nike's lost share. If the rotation is toward sport-performance crossover (Adidas's lane), Lululemon's premium athleisure lane risks being bypassed in the upper-funnel conversation.
2Lululemon's hold is structural, not growing.12.2% SoS is consistent across both windows. Strategy should focus on deepening loyalty and lifetime value rather than top-of-funnel acquisition that merely defends existing share.
3P.E Nation's 3-month acceleration is a conquest window.+100% relative SoS growth in 3 months from 1.5% to 3.0%. The most likely brand to chip into Lululemon's premium AU-native positioning. Targeted paid search on Lululemon's key brand terms now, before P.E Nation builds further momentum.
4October counter-seasonal opportunity.Lululemon's lowest SoS point is October. Media weight in September-October is cheaper (lower auction competition). A brand-building campaign in this window builds base SoS ahead of the November peak.
