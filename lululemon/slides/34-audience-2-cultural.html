Six cultural signals make Audience 2 Lululemon’s most strategically significant growth cohort in Australia.

Audience 2 · 6 signals
Verified
Signal 01

Menswear growth as Lululemon’s strategic priority.

Lululemon’s menswear line has been expanding at a CAGR of nearly 20% since 2021. The brand has moved aggressively on performance shorts (ABC, Commission), base layers and training kit to capture male fitness consumers previously defaulting to Nike or Under Armour. In Australia, the men’s range is increasingly visible in premium gym and run-club contexts.

businessmodelanalyst.com Verified
Signal 02

Run club as social identity movement.

Community fitness, specifically run club culture, has accelerated post-COVID as a social reintegration mechanism for urban professional men. Australia’s fitness industry data shows group fitness and community events as key drivers of male gym retention. Run clubs in Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane are now social and professional networking events as much as exercise formats.

Signal 03

Premium athleisure shifting men’s wardrobe logic.

The boundary between gym kit and casual wear is collapsing for men as it has for women. Lululemon’s ABC (Anti-Ball Crushing) pant is a clear signal: designed as dress-casual but marketed as gym-capable. Australian male consumers in professional roles are increasingly wearing premium performance clothing to offices, cafes and social events.

IMARC Australia Activewear; ragtrader.com.au Verified
Signal 04

Strength training resurgence among professional men.

Traditional strength training ranks 4th in AUSactive’s 2025-26 national survey, driven by men aged 25 to 45 adopting structured weightlifting, Crossfit and hybrid training. This cohort spends on quality training shorts, compression layers and versatile warmup gear. Performance demands align well with Lululemon’s metal vent tech and training short range.

Signal 05

High-net-worth individual inflow driving premium market.

Australia recorded one of the highest net inflows of High-Net-Worth Individuals globally, at 2,500 as of 2024. This immigration cohort is pre-adapted to premium international brands and accelerates the addressable market for Lululemon’s men’s range in Sydney and Melbourne specifically. CBRE identifies this as a meaningful driver of premium retail growth.

CBRE via commo.com.au Verified
Signal 06

Wearable tech and performance data integration.

Wearable technology ranks 5th in AUSactive’s national fitness trends survey. The male performance enthusiast is data-driven: he tracks resting heart rate, VO2 max, recovery and sleep. Lululemon’s community platform and performance ecosystem extends the brand into a data context that resonates with this audience’s quantified-self mindset.
