Part 05 · Competitive Context · GEO recommendations

Three moves to lift GEO presence. Fix the AU review signal first: it is the single query where an AI gives a negative recommendation.

From the GEO extension scan
Probable
Lululemon's PRIMARY GEO positions today are earned through third-party editorial and category reputation. AI models cite Women's Weekly, Broadsheet, and comparison sites, not lululemon.com.au. The BURIED positions (Q5, Q6) are driven by AU review platform scores that Lululemon does not own or manage. ProductReview.com.au (1.8/5, 146 reviews) is the authoritative direct-scrape signal. Trustpilot AU was bot-blocked during the scan and its score is unverified. Until the AU review-score signal is repaired, any AI asked about Lululemon's value in Australia will surface a negative recommendation.
Recommendation01

Launch a dedicated AU customer advocacy programme targeting ProductReview and Trustpilot.

ProductReview.com.au (1.8/5, 146 reviews, direct scrape VERIFIED) is the first signal any AI model returns on "is Lululemon worth it in Australia." Trustpilot AU returned HTTP 403 on direct scrape -- score unverified. Until ProductReview scores lift above 3.5, the Q5 BURIED position cannot be repaired by any content strategy. Priority action: post-purchase AU-specific review flow targeting verified buyers, with specific focus on ProductReview.com.au. Pair with a rapid-response CS programme to address pilling and wait-time complaints that currently dominate.

Q5Lululemon Australia review worth it 2026
Expected lift: Verified need. No content strategy fixes this query until scores lift. Estimated minimum 3-6 months of active advocacy to move the aggregate above 3.5.
Recommendation02

Build AU-native yoga content to close the Lorna Jane gap on premium yoga queries.

For "premium yoga pants Australia" queries, AU-native brands (Lorna Jane, Nimble, Stax) dominate AI-visible editorial because they are coded as "Australian." Lululemon is absent from the top 3 in yoga-specific AU roundups despite being the global category leader. A single "Lululemon Align vs AU competitors for hot yoga" article on a high-DA AU site (Broadsheet, RUSSH, Women's Weekly) would shift the GEO signal on yoga-specific AU queries. Pitch to AU editorial as a product placement, not a paid ad.

Targets
Q2Premium yoga pants Australia best brands
Expected lift: Probable. Could move Q2 from Visible-Secondary to Visible-Primary for yoga-specific AU queries.
Recommendation03

Reclaim the "alternatives" query with a cost-per-wear content strategy.

The "lululemon alternatives Australia" query (Q6) is high-volume and Lululemon is currently the benchmark being abandoned rather than defended. Lorna Jane captures most of this traffic as the named alternative. A brand-owned page or editorial pitch targeting "why Lululemon is still the best value long-term" with cost-per-wear analysis, longevity data, and repair/guarantee information would shift AI visibility from "alternatives" framing to "worth it" framing. Cross-link to Recommendation 01 to reinforce the narrative with improved review scores.

Targets
Q6Lululemon alternatives Australia cheaper activewear
Opportunity: Lorna Jane currently captures this query. Cost-per-wear framing is an uncontested angle in AU content. Probable to shift from Buried to Secondary within two quarters if paired with improved review scores.
