The brand

A turnaround brand deploying media in micro-doses, not mass campaigns.

Under Armour is a global performance sportswear brand recognised for high-performance fabric technology and athlete sponsorships, ranked alongside Nike, Adidas, and Puma as a major international player in the AU activewear market. Globally, the brand is in a multi-year turnaround under CEO Kevin Plank, with marketing spend deliberately kept flat and deployed in "micro doses" rather than mass campaigns.

In Australia, Under Armour's 2024 partnership with ISC Sport reaches 800,000+ athletes across 2,500+ clubs, signalling a grassroots growth strategy targeting 18-24 year old athletes. Above-the-line AU presence remains minimal versus Nike and Adidas.

Why it matters to Lululemon

Under Armour is not a direct threat to Lululemon's premium athleisure positioning, but its ISC Sport grassroots club strategy could seed loyalty with younger performance athletes before Lululemon builds relationships there. Share of voice significantly lags market potential, placing it firmly in the under-indexed category. Stalled at 2.9% SoS.
