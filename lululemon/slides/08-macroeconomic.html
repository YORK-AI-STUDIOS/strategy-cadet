05

Three macroeconomic forces shape the demand environment for premium activewear in Australia.

Synthesis
Verified
01 · Force

Consumer confidence at a low, goods inflation elevated.

68.5
AU consumer confidence index, March 2026. Goods inflation running at 5.5% YoY, compressing discretionary spend.

Only 15% of Australians report being financially better off than a year ago. With goods inflation at 5.5% year-on-year and wages growth insufficient to offset cost-of-living pressure, discretionary spending on premium apparel faces sustained headwinds.

Price sensitivity is structurally elevated: 86% of AU consumers now prioritise price in purchasing decisions. Lululemon must work harder to justify premium price points with verified quality and lifestyle value, not assumed brand cachet.

Reads as increased pressure on new customer acquisition at Lululemon’s price tier; loyal existing customers are more resilient but the top-of-funnel challenge is real.
Verified
Source: ABS CPI Australia March 2026.
02 · Force

Online spending surge accelerating DTC advantage.

+14% YoY
AU online spending growth 2025, reaching AUD 82.6 billion total. Fashion and apparel at 21.38% of e-commerce share.

The 14% surge in Australian online spending in 2025 is accelerating the structural shift of apparel revenue into DTC digital channels. Brands with strong e-commerce infrastructure and data-led personalisation are capturing margin and customer relationships that wholesale cannot replicate.

Lululemon’s DTC capability is a competitive premium. The AU e-commerce surge compounds this advantage and rewards investment in digital experience, fit tools, and personalised retention programs.

Reinforces the case for Lululemon to invest in AU e-commerce personalisation and a fit-diagnostic tool that extends the in-store educator model to digital-first customers.
Verified
Source: auspost.com.au AU Post E-Commerce Industry Report 2025.
03 · Force

US tariff fallout redirecting fast-fashion volumes to Australia.

AUD 3B+
Estimated SHEIN and Temu annual AU revenue. Trump-era US tariff enforcement has pushed ultra-cheap imports toward tariff-lenient markets including Australia.

The Trump-era US tariff crackdown on low-value e-commerce imports has redirected SHEIN and Temu volumes toward markets with lenient import tax rules, Australia chief among them. This amplifies the fast-fashion threat and continues to depress category average selling prices in the sub-premium tier.

Lululemon’s premium positioning is structurally insulated from direct comparison, but the volume and visibility of ultra-cheap alternatives raises the bar for justifying any premium price point to newer, less brand-loyal segments.

Raises the urgency of Lululemon’s technical differentiation narrative: fabric science and product longevity must be communicated as rational, not just aspirational, purchase justifications.

Source: texfash.com SHEIN, Temu and the tariff fallout analysis, 2025.
