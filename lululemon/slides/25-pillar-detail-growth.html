Belonging You Can Sweat Your Way Into.

An open community with a behavioural entry bar: show up, move, repeat.

The ambassador network, studio partnerships, and SweatLife event calendar operate as an open community with one entry condition: show up. Growth comes from expanding who feels invited without diluting the earned-belonging dynamic that makes the community credible.

The Lewis Hamilton partnership (confirmed February 2025, AU Grand Prix Emporium Melbourne activation March 2026), the “Together We Grow” APAC campaign (Sept-Nov 2025, one million minutes of movement target), and the Brookfield Place Sydney flagship (July 2025) are the three biggest growth infrastructure moves of the current period. The plan to double the Australian business (confirmed December 2025 franchise expansion announcement) is the financial expression of this pillar.

What this looks like in executionFour tactical moves, sequenced for the next twelve months
EX 01

Regional ambassador infrastructure build.

Close the gap between brand awareness and genuine community belonging in Brisbane, Perth, and Adelaide. Locally contextualised SweatLife events rather than centralised national programming.

EX 02

Men’s community layer via run clubs and training events.

The ABC pant has a men’s foothold. The community infrastructure skews toward female-coded studio culture. Run clubs and male ambassador profiles build the men’s belonging layer without product repositioning.

EX 03

UGC-forward social amplifying real community movement.

Community members’ own movement content amplified via @lululemonausnz (405K followers). Reduces production cost and increases credibility simultaneously. Earned belonging is most legible when customers show it themselves.

EX 04

Running category community activation.

The running category pushed into AU market (June 2025, Ragtrader). Run clubs are the natural community activation format for the running category and the clearest bridge to new male and Gen Z audiences.
