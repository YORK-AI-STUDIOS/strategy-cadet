Six cultural signals shape what this audience expects from an activewear brand.

Signal 01

The Pilates boom as identity marker.

Pilates has overtaken HIIT and cardio as the defining fitness activity for Australian women in this age group, ranking number 2 in the AUSactive 2025-26 national fitness trends survey. In Victoria, women make up more than 90% of Pilates participants. Reformer Pilates studios have become social hubs, as much about community and self-expression as physical results. Premium kit worn to the studio is an extension of this identity.

Signal 02

Studio-to-street dressing as the new norm.

Australian consumers increasingly wear activewear for casual outings, working from home, and social engagements. The line between workout wear and workwear is dissolving in inner-city contexts. Lululemon’s crossover aesthetic (Align leggings and Define jacket) sits precisely at this intersection. IMARC research confirms activewear’s expansion beyond fitness contexts.

IMARC Australia Activewear Market Report Verified
Signal 03

Mental health as fitness motivation.

Exercise for mental health ranked number 3 in AUSactive’s national survey. Australian women in their 30s and 40s are reframing fitness away from aesthetics and toward stress management, emotional regulation, and cognitive performance. This cohort is receptive to brand messaging around mental clarity and reset, not weight loss or appearance.

Signal 04

Premium athleisure replacing premium fashion.

Consumers are shifting discretionary spend from traditional premium fashion toward premium athleisure. The Australian activewear market reached USD 8.12 billion in 2024, growing at a CAGR of 5.11% toward USD 10.91 billion by 2030. Women remain the dominant segment. FashionNetwork confirms spending habits reflect a deliberate value hierarchy shift.

Research and Markets; FashionNetwork Verified
Signal 05

Yoga participation at scale, skewing affluent.

Roy Morgan data shows 1.3 million Australians regularly practice yoga, with women comprising over 70% of participants. More than 50% of yoga and Pilates participants sit in the top two socioeconomic quintiles, directly overlapping with Lululemon’s price tolerance zone. This is not a fringe activity: it is a mainstream affluent-female behaviour.

Roy Morgan Research Probable
Signal 06

Athleisure precincts signalling lifestyle positioning.

Premium suburban retail precincts (Melbourne’s Armadale, featuring Lululemon alongside Nimble, Style Runner and 2XU) reflect a deliberate co-location strategy. Brands are expanding into premium suburban locations to meet customers in their own neighbourhoods rather than CBD hubs. CBRE identifies this as a reshaping of Australia’s retail market.
