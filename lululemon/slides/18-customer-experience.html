01 · How customers should feel

Seen, capable, and part of something.

·Seen. ·Capable. ·Genuinely welcomed.

The brand wants customers to feel that their investment in themselves is recognised and supported, that the product performs without getting in the way, and that the community around the brand is genuinely welcoming rather than aspirationally exclusive.

From Brand DNA prompt 14.
02 · What makes the experience unique

A studio consultation, not a retail visit.

Store staff are hired for their lifestyle alignment and trained to diagnose fit and function needs, not to close a transaction. The in-store experience mirrors the community philosophy: advice-led, warm, and genuinely product-knowledgeable.

AU review data confirms this holds in flagship stores: knowledgeable, enthusiastic, sizing-savvy staff are the brand’s clearest competitive strength in market. Melbourne Outlet is the known exception where service quality breaks down.

The online channel does not replicate the consultative quality. The educator model is the in-store asset that digital currently fails to extend.

03 · Customer service promise

Five words or fewer, the promise it has to live up to.

“Feel it. Know it works.”

Verbatim from Brand DNA prompt 16. The promise is sensory and functional: customers should be able to feel the product difference and trust it will perform.

The 1.4-star online store rating and 60%+ returns-policy complaint rate (AU ProductReview) suggest the promise is currently under-delivered in the digital channel, not the physical one. The gap is the strategic opening.
