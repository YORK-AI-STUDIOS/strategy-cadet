Strategy Cadet · How it works · What sets it apart

Two structural moats. Four confidence tags. Zero pretending.

Single-pass AI tools produce one output and hope it holds up. Trouble is, a fluent hallucination reads exactly like a verified fact, and humans are easily fooled. Cadet adjudicates its own work, then labels every claim with the evidence behind it.
1 Moat · Adjudication-Grade Goal Validation

Every recommended goal is tested against three failure modes before it ships.

Most tools stop at proposing a goal. Cadet runs three independent tests. Any goal that fails one is reworked before the report ships.

Test 01
Market reality
Is the addressable market large enough to support the goal?
Test 02
Competitive density
Is the positioning still available, or is it already owned?
Test 03
Brand capability
Does the brand have the assets to deliver on this goal?
2 Moat · Phase 3 Cross-Reference Verification

Every factual claim is independently re-verified before the report is built.

Every factual claim is extracted and re-checked against a second authoritative source. Discrepancies are flagged for operator verification.

Caught in a recent run

"A Big-Four firm was named as the auditor instead of the correct Big-Four firm. The kind of error any reader in financial services would spot."

Corrected before the report shipped. That class of error never reaches the client.

Four confidence tags. Colour-coded on every claim.

Tagged at generation · upgraded only after explicit re-research
Verified Strongest
Externally confirmed via authoritative source.
Multiple independent sources, or one definitive primary record, confirm the claim. Defensible in a boardroom without further audit.
Probable Strong
Externally sourced but partial.
Single credible source, logical inference from verified adjacent data, or industry consensus. Strong, but worth checking before a high-stakes commitment.
Estimated Calculated
Numeric estimate with stated assumptions.
A figure computed from available data because the exact number is not externally published. Every input and assumption disclosed alongside.
Hypothesised Working
AI synthesis, working hypothesis.
A possibility reasoned from available signals, not directly evidenced. Surfaces a thread worth pulling. Never asserts itself as fact.
Honest confidence, throughout.
Your strategist sees exactly what's grounded research, what's a calculated estimate, and what's inventive synthesis worth stress-testing. Cadet labels its work. The strategist makes the call.
